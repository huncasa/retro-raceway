The Last RWD Rally Car to Win the WRC

The Lancia 037 is a legendary rally car that made a significant impact in the world of motorsports during the 1980s. It was developed by Lancia to compete in the World Rally Championship, and it was designed from the ground up to be a dominant force on the rally stages. The Lancia 037 is an icon of rallying and a symbol of the golden era of the sport. The Lancia 037 was first introduced in 1982 and was designed by Abarth, Lancia's racing division. The car was based on the Lancia Beta Montecarlo, but it featured significant modifications to its suspension, chassis, and engine. The 037 was built using a tubular steel spaceframe chassis, which made the car lightweight and agile. The car was fitted with a mid-mounted engine, which gave it excellent weight distribution and handling characteristics. The Lancia 037 was powered by a 2.0-liter, four-cylinder engine that was turbocharged and intercooled. The engine was capable of producing up to 325 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, which made the car incredibly fast and powerful. The car was fitted with a five-speed manual transmission that was designed to handle the engine's immense power. The Lancia 037 was a rear-wheel-drive car, and it was fitted with a limited-slip differential to improve traction and handling. The car's suspension system was designed to be adjustable, which allowed the driver to fine-tune the car's handling characteristics to suit different driving conditions. The car was fitted with large ventilated disc brakes on all four wheels, which provided excellent stopping power. The Lancia 037's exterior design was heavily influenced by its function as a rally car. The Lancia 037 was a dominant force in rallying for several years, and it paved the way for the development of modern rally cars. The Lancia 037's legacy in rallying is secure, and the car is highly sought after by collectors and enthusiasts today.
