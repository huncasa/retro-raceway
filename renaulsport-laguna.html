A Dominant Force in Touring Car Racing

The Renault Sport Laguna is a touring car that was produced by Renault Sport in the late 1990s for competition in the Super Touring category. Based on the Renault Laguna road car, the Renault Sport Laguna was designed and built to compete against other Super Touring cars such as the BMW 320i, Audi A4, and Volvo S40. The Renault Sport Laguna was powered by a 2.0-liter, 16-valve engine that produced approximately 280 horsepower. The engine was based on the production engine found in the Renault Laguna road car, but was extensively modified for racing. The Renault Sport Laguna also featured a six-speed sequential gearbox, which was operated by paddles mounted behind the steering wheel. The Renault Sport Laguna made its debut in the 1998 British Touring Car Championship, driven by Alain Menu and Jason Plato. The car was immediately competitive, and Plato won the first race of the season in the car. The car also won 13 out of the 26 races in the 1999 BTCC season, including a one-two finish at the season finale at Silverstone. The Renault Sport Laguna also competed in other touring car championships around the world, including the French Super Touring Championship and the European Super Touring Cup.
