Mitsubishi Lancer Evo VI

A Legendary Rally Car

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI, also known as the Evo VI, is a high-performance rally car that was produced by Mitsubishi Motors from 1999 to 2001. It was the sixth generation of the Lancer Evolution series and was designed to compete in the World Rally Championship (WRC) and other rally events. The Evo VI was based on the Mitsubishi Lancer sedan, but it had significant modifications to make it suitable for rallying. The car had a lightweight body, a stiffened chassis, and a powerful engine. It was equipped with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produced up to 276 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque. The engine was mated to a five-speed manual transmission and a sophisticated all-wheel-drive system that gave the car exceptional traction on both tarmac and gravel roads. One of the most notable features of the Evo VI was its advanced active yaw control (AYC) system, which helped to improve the car's handling and stability. The AYC system used sensors and electronic controls to distribute power between the front and rear wheels, and between the left and right wheels, to ensure maximum grip in all driving conditions. The Evo VI was highly successful in the WRC, winning the Constructors' Championship in 1999 and 2001, and the Drivers' Championship in 2001 with driver Tommi Makinen. It was also a popular choice among privateer teams and amateur rally enthusiasts, who appreciated its performance and reliability.

From Daily Driver to Rally Champion

In addition to its success in rally competition, the Evo VI also gained a reputation as a high-performance road car. Its potent engine, all-wheel-drive system, and advanced handling features made it a favorite among driving enthusiasts who wanted a car that could handle both the track and the street. Overall, the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI is a legendary rally car that has left a lasting impact on the automotive world. Its advanced technology, impressive performance, and championship-winning pedigree make it a true icon of the rally scene.
