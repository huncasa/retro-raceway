Lancia Stratos

Unleashing the Beast on the Track

The Lancia Stratos is a rally car that was produced by the Italian automaker Lancia in the mid-1970s. It was designed by Bertone and developed by Lancia's competition department, which was headed by Cesare Fiorio. The Stratos was a purpose-built rally car that was designed to win the World Rally Championship, which it did in 1974, 1975, and 1976. The Stratos was powered by a mid-mounted 2.4-liter V6 engine that was derived from the Ferrari Dino. It produced 190 horsepower in its early versions and up to 280 horsepower in its later versions. The engine was mated to a five-speed manual transmission that sent power to the rear wheels. The Stratos was known for its distinctive wedge shape, which was designed to maximize downforce and stability at high speeds. It had a short wheelbase and a wide track, which made it very agile and responsive. The Stratos also had a lightweight fiberglass body, which contributed to its impressive performance.

Breaking Records

The Stratos competed in rally events all over the world, and it was particularly successful on tarmac stages, where its handling and stability made it almost unbeatable. It was driven by some of the greatest rally drivers of the era, including Sandro Munari, Markku Alén, and Björn Waldegård. The Stratos was also notable for its innovative design and engineering. It was one of the first rally cars to use a spaceframe chassis, which was both lightweight and stiff. It also featured a unique suspension system that used double wishbones at the front and a semi-trailing arm setup at the rear. Despite its success on the rally stages, the Stratos was not a commercial success for Lancia. Only 492 road-going versions were produced, and they were expensive and difficult to maintain. However, the Stratos remains one of the most iconic rally cars of all time, and it continues to inspire car enthusiasts and rally fans around the world.
