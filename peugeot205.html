Peugeot 205 T16

The French Rally Icon

The Peugeot 205 T16 is a legendary Group B rally car that was built in the mid-1980s with the aim of winning the World Rally Championship. It was a highly advanced machine that was built to compete against some of the best rally cars of its time, and it achieved great success in the hands of some of the greatest drivers in rally history. The 205 T16 was born out of Peugeot's desire to win the World Rally Championship, and it was built to comply with the Group B regulations that were in place at the time. The car was based on the 205 road car, but it was significantly modified to become a rally monster. It featured a mid-mounted engine, four-wheel drive, and a highly aerodynamic body that was designed to generate maximum downforce at high speeds. Under the hood, the 205 T16 was powered by a 1.8-liter turbocharged engine that was capable of producing over 500 horsepower in its final iteration. The car's power was sent to all four wheels through a five-speed manual transmission, which allowed it to achieve incredible acceleration and top speeds.

From Group B Regulations to Championship Success

In competition, the Peugeot 205 T16 proved to be a dominant force, winning two World Rally Championships in 1985 and 1986, and numerous other events throughout its career. It was driven by some of the greatest rally drivers of all time, including Ari Vatanen, Timo Salonen, and Juha Kankkunen. Despite its success, the 205 T16's career was cut short by the cancellation of Group B in 1986, which was due to safety concerns after a series of fatal accidents. As a result, the car was forced into early retirement, but its legacy lived on as one of the greatest rally cars of all time. Today, the Peugeot 205 T16 is a highly sought-after collector's car, and it remains a symbol of Peugeot's rally dominance during the 1980s. It is a car that is highly respected by rally fans and drivers alike, and it will forever be remembered as a true icon of the sport.
