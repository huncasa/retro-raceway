Ford RS200

Rally Success

The Ford RS200 was a rally car produced by the American automaker Ford during the mid-1980s. It was a purpose-built Group B rally car designed to compete in the World Rally Championship, and it was intended to be a successor to the Ford Escort RS1800. However, the RS200 never achieved the same level of success as its predecessor due to the early cancellation of Group B rallying. The RS200 was based on a mid-engined, four-wheel drive layout that was developed by British company, Ferguson Formula. Power came from a 1.8-liter, turbocharged engine that was capable of producing up to 450 horsepower in its final evolution. This made it one of the most powerful rally cars of its time, and it was capable of reaching speeds of up to 140 mph. The RS200 was designed to be both lightweight and durable, and it featured a lightweight fiberglass body mounted on a tubular spaceframe chassis. The suspension was also designed for maximum performance, with adjustable dampers, anti-roll bars, and a four-wheel independent suspension system.

The RS200's Legacy

Despite its impressive performance, the RS200's competitive career was cut short due to the cancellation of Group B rallying in 1986. However, the car remained popular among rally fans and enthusiasts due to its unique design and impressive performance capabilities. In addition to its rally career, the RS200 also saw limited success in other motorsports, including hillclimbs and circuit racing. The car's durability and performance made it a popular choice for these events, and it continued to be used in motorsports throughout the 1990s. Today, the RS200 is considered a highly sought-after and rare collector's item, with only 200 examples ever produced. Its unique design and impressive performance capabilities continue to make it a popular choice among rally fans and collectors alike.
