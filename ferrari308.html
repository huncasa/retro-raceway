Ferrari 308

The Italian Icon of the 70s and 80s Racing Scene

The Ferrari 308 is a classic Italian sports car that was produced between 1975 and 1985. It was the successor to the Dino 246 GT and was designed by Pininfarina. The Ferrari 308 was a popular choice for enthusiasts who wanted a high-performance sports car that looked great and handled well. However, many people don't know that the Ferrari 308 also had a successful racing career, particularly in the Group 4 and Group B rally classes. One of the things that made the Ferrari 308 so appealing was its design. It had a sleek and low profile, with a wedge shape that was popular in the 1970s. The body was made of fiberglass, which made it lightweight and nimble on the road. The car featured a 3.0-liter V8 engine that produced 240 horsepower, which was plenty for a car that weighed less than 2,800 pounds. The engine was mounted transversely in the middle of the car, which helped to improve its balance and handling. In addition to its impressive performance on the road, the Ferrari 308 was also successful in racing. The car competed in various championships throughout the 1970s and 1980s, including the World Rally Championship and the European Hill Climb Championship. In Group 4 racing, the Ferrari 308 won the 1978 Tour de France Automobile and the 1979 Rallye Automobile de Monte-Carlo. It also had success in Group B rally racing, with a modified version of the car winning the 1981 Tour de France Automobile and the 1982 Rallye Automobile de Monte-Carlo.
